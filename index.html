About Me

Water resources engineer by training, turned geospatial data scientist through hands-on work in flood modeling, hydropower optimization, and climate risk analytics.

I specialize in building end-to-end spatial workflows—from satellite imagery processing and hydrological simulation to database architecture and API deployment. My background in hydraulic engineering provides domain expertise that shapes how I approach geospatial problems: with rigorous attention to physical processes and real-world constraints.

I've worked across research institutes (INRAe), energy companies (ENGIE/CNR), catastrophe risk startups (REOR20), and public infrastructure agencies (ANBT). This cross-sector experience taught me to bridge scientific rigor with operational requirements, translating complex spatial analyses into actionable outputs for engineers, planners, and decision-makers.
